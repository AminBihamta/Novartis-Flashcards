Case Study: Malaysia

EASy: Enhancing Access to Sybrava via Buy Now Pay Later [BNPL]

Background

  • Since its launch, Sybrava has garnered over 900 patients, with two-thirds enrolling in the Sybrava Patient Support Program (PSP), known as ADOPT. However, a significant dropout rate of 63% has been observed from the 3rd dose onward.
  • Market research insights from patients indicate that cost is a major factor influencing adherence. Patients are more inclined to continue treatment if the monthly cost is below a threshold of RM XX, in contrast to the current price range is between RMXX to RMXX per injection for Sybrava.
  • To address this issue, the team has been identifying other financial options to improve patient access following the discontinuation of PSP in January 2024.

Project Goal

  • This program aims to collaborate with Pine Lab and the KPJ Healthcare group to offer the installment plan as an option for outpatients who are prescribed but face challenges with the high upfront payment. Roll-out date : 2nd May 2025.
  • Pine Lab is a fintech company available in providing credit card facilities at all KPJ hospitals. They offer installment plans for major credit card companies, but currently, these plans are only available for in-patient treatment costs, while most Sybrava prescriptions are for outpatient consultations.
  • The team is piloting a program targeting the 2 KPJ hospitals (Damansara Specialist, and Tawakal Hospital) with the highest credit card utilization. Once streamlined, the program will expand to all KPJ hospitals nationwide and, if successful, to other private hospitals in Malaysia.

Key Learnings

The program will officially kick off on May 2nd at the two pilot hospitals, thus, limited learnings at this point.

Some learnings while preparing for this program:
Partnering with a FinTech that already provides credit card facilities expedited buy-in from KPJ group; nevertheless, extended timelines for legal and data protection alignments among all parties should be anticipated.
