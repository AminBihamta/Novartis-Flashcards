Case Study: Australia

Novartis Connect: A centralised self-pay program broadening patient and healthcare institution access across the full pharmaceutical portfolio

Background

  • Australia is an established market with broad government funding. The private market opportunity represents a minor share (approx. 0.2%) of business for the industry.
  • The single payer system is experiencing a significant delay between registration and reimbursement, interrupting patient access to innovative medicines.
  • There are inconsistencies across the self-pay market, causing variability in patient experience for those that rely on private prescriptions, including variances in pricing, script fulfilment, payment options, and delivery and collection partners.

Project Goal

  • Provide sustained access to our medicines from 'day zero' through the private market channel, reaching 620+ pharmacies nation-wide and targeting 1000+ in late 2025.
  • Streamline and simplify the patient experience to make it easier and convenient for patients to access Novartis medications with a private prescription.
  • Ensure consistent pricing and experiment with discounting mechanisms for Novartis private medications across enabled pharmacies and healthcare institutions to lower cost as a barrier to entry.

Key Learnings

Discount mechanisms must ensure price confidentiality, while also providing transparency and value optimization to patients.

Only a few specialised providers can offer online ordering and collection services, cold chain and hospital delivery. There is not one pharmacy group that covers the whole of Australia creating inconsistencies in patient experience.

Digital prescriptions and eCommerce is not a routine way to access medicines in Australia, for patients or clinicians.
