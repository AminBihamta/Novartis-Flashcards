Case Study: India

Leveraging Private Health Insurance to improve access for Sybrava (Leqvio)

Background

Implemented in April 2024, the program aimed to improve access to innovative medicines like Sybrava (Leqvio) in India by leveraging Private Insurance. Initially, low awareness of new insurance guidelines among HCPs, patients, TPAs, and hospital administrators led to a 70% claim rejection rate.

Project Goal

The project aimed to reduce Sybrava insurance claim rejections by educating and engaging all key PHI stakeholders — Healthcare Providers (HCPs), Insurers, TPAs, Patients, and Hospital Administrators—to enable early, sustained access and improve patient outcomes while easing the self-pay burden.

Key Learnings

Education & Engagement

Intensive awareness campaigns significantly enhanced confidence among HCPs and insurance executives, leading to more accurate and efficient claims processing.

Stakeholder Collaboration

Proactive engagement across the PHI ecosystem, including TPAs, helped overcome biases and fostered trust and cooperation.

Impact Measurement

Sybrava claim rejection rates dropped from 70% in April 2024 to 30% by December 2024, demonstrating the program’s effectiveness.
